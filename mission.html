✦ Our Story

Born from
a Broken System

Leave a Seed was founded in 2018 when our founder, James Okafor, returned home to rural Mississippi to find his childhood neighborhood had become a food desert — the nearest grocery store a 40-minute drive away.

He began distributing seed packets and growing guides at the local library. Within a season, 60 families were growing food in their backyards, on their porches, and in empty lots. The food bank lines shortened. Community pride grew.

That moment showed us what we now know to be true: seeds and knowledge are among the most powerful anti-hunger tools we have. Leave a Seed exists to scale that moment to every food desert in America.

✦ Our Journey

From One Seed
to a Movement

2018

The First Seed Packet

Founder James Okafor distributes 200 seed packets with hand-written growing guides at a library in Mississippi. 60 families sign up in the first week.

2019

The Marketplace Launches

Leave a Seed goes online, connecting 12 local seed vendors with growers across 4 states. 5% of every sale is pledged to hunger relief from day one.

2021

100 Vendor Milestone & Education Platform

We reach 100 verified vendors and launch our free education platform — the first to pair seed purchases with localized growing guides and planting calendars.

2023

50 Food Bank Partnerships

We formalize partnerships with 50+ food banks and community garden networks, enabling direct seed donations to over 200 food desert zip codes across America.

2025

1 Million Meals Milestone

Our community officially crosses the 1 million meals enabled milestone. 500+ vendors, 12,000+ seed varieties, and a growing movement of growers who give back.
